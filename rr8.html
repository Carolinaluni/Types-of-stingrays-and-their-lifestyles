a list of things to buy before the puppy arrives

  • Collar and leash - for walks and identification purposes
  • ID tag - with your puppy's name and your contact information
  • Food and water bowls - make sure they are the appropriate size for your puppy
  • High-quality puppy food - check with your veterinarian for recommendations on the best food for your puppy's breed and age
  • Treats - for training and rewarding good behavior
  • Puppy training pads - for potty training
  • Crate or carrier - for transportation and safe confinement when necessary
  • Bed - for sleeping and relaxing
  • Toys - for play and mental stimulation
  • Grooming supplies - such as a brush, shampoo, and nail clippers
  • Stain and odor remover - for accidents during potty training
  • Poop bags - for picking up after your puppy during walks
  • Baby gates - for keeping your puppy in certain areas of the house
  • First-aid kit - including items such as antiseptic, bandages, and tweezers
  • Puppy-proofing supplies - to keep your puppy safe and prevent damage to your home, such as electrical cord covers, cabinet locks, and garbage cans with lids.
